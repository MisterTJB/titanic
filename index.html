The Titanic Dataset

The purpose of our lesson today is to train an Artificial Neural Network to categorise Titanic passengers as survivors or non-survivors based on the information that can be retrieved from the ship's manifest. Our ultimate goal is to use our Neural Net to determine if Jack and Rose — had they been real passengers — would have survived.

If you're reading this, then you have everything that you need.

What's in the Manifest?

Glad you asked! There are 11 columns in the dataset. If you have Excel on your computer, you can open titanic_dataset.csv to see what it looks like.

  • Survived — did the passenger survive?
  • Pclass — what class were they in?
  • Name — their name (and title)
  • Sex — male or female
  • Age — in years
  • SibSp — the number of family members ([Sib]lings and [Sp]ouse) that the person was travelling with
  • Parch — the number of parents that children were travelling with, or children that parents were travelling with
  • Ticket number — a code that no one understands
  • Fare — the amount paid for the ticket
  • Cabin — their room number
  • Embarked — [C]herbourg (France), [S]outhampton (England), or [Q]ueenstown (Ireland)
